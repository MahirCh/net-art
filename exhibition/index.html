Files Declassified- Life Unprivatized

Curated by Mahir Chaudhry

The following three pieces of net art show a choice that many today may perhaps do unknowingly- showing their life online! Today, thousands of people head to social media to share their life with others all across the globe, but did you know that before Facebook, Instagram, and Tiktok, some people willingly shared not just silly or educational videos or photos online, but their entire life? These three pieces of net art largely rely on providing viewers with a new “lens” with which they can view information. For instance, iPhone Live provided viewers with a window into the life of Osterhoff, giving them a hint of how he used his phone over the period of a year. Very similarly, Syms gave viewers a window into her life by providing them with her three-year period search history. Eva and Franco Mattes, like Osterhoff and Syms, too gave viewers a new perspective - they made their computer information public to viewers just how it is! Viewers could read, download, and copy all data, including bank statements, giving an entirely new sort of openness to the public and letting go of privacy that others may hold very dear to!


Life Sharing (2000 - 2003)- Eva and Franco Mattes

Couple Eva and Franco Mattes made all of their computer history and files available online to read, copy, and download over a three year period of 2000 to 2003.


EverythingIveEverWantedtoKnow.com (2004-2007) - Martine Syms

Syms gathered together her search history on Google over a period of three years (2004-2007) as a website for others to view.


iPhone Live (2012-2013) - Johannes P. Osterhoff

Osterhoff posted screenshots of his phone activity over a period of one year.
